About Me

I am an assistant professor at the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, the University of Hong Kong, and the Principal Investigator (PI) of the Wireless Information & Intelligence (WILL) lab. I obtained my Ph.D. degree in electrical and computer engineering from the University of Florida in 2022, supervised by Prof. Yuguang Fang, and my B.Eng. degree from Southwest Jiaotong University, Chengdu, China, in 2017, supervised by Prof. Gang Liu. My research interests include wireless networks, edge computing, distributed learning, and network security.

Research Interests

My primary research interest lies in the optimization of distributed AI in (wireless) networked systems, including

  • Wireless networking for computing/machine learning

    • Wireless split learning
    • Wireless federated learning
    • Edge inference
    • Mobile edge computing

  • Machine learning for wireless networking

    • Reinforcement learning

  • Security and privacy in distributed learning

    • Privacy-preserving split/federated learning
